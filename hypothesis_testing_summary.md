# Hypothesis Testing Summary

## Business Hypothesis
The High Value customer segment has a significantly higher Average Order
Value (AOV) than the rest of the customer base.

## Hypotheses
- **H0 (Null):** No significant difference in AOV between High Value and other customers.
- **H1 (Alternative):** High Value customers have a significantly higher AOV than other customers.

## Test Used
Independent samples T-test (Welch's, unequal variance), α = 0.05

## Results
| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| High Value mean AOV | ₹326,342.33 |
| Other segments mean AOV | ₹92,663.72 |
| T-statistic | 42.43 |
| P-value | < 0.00001 |
| 95% Confidence Interval | [₹222,883.24, ₹244,474.00] |

## Conclusion
**Reject H0.** With a p-value far below 0.05 (p < 0.00001), the difference
in Average Order Value between the High Value segment and the rest of the
customer base is statistically significant — not due to random chance.
High Value customers spend on average over ₹233K more per order than other
customers, confirming this segment as a genuinely distinct, high-priority
customer group.

## Revenue Contribution by Segment
| Segment | Share of Total Revenue |
|---|---|
| High Value | 46.8% |
| Medium Value | 35.0% |
| Low Value | 18.1% |

## Business Recommendation
Launch a dedicated loyalty/retention program for the High Value segment.
This group drives nearly half (46.8%) of total revenue with a
statistically confirmed AOV roughly 3.5x higher than other customers.
Protecting and growing this segment should be a top business priority.
